Accessibility Features for AAC

Built-in device features can make AAC easier. Availability depends on the device model and OS.

Apple (iOS / iPadOS) Features

Eye Tracking

Control the device using only eye movements.

Requires: iPadOS 18+ / iOS 18+ AND iPhone 12+ or iPads: A14 chip+ (iPad 10+, Air 4+, Mini 6+, Pro 2018+).

Personal Voice

Create a synthesized voice that sounds like you by recording sample phrases.

Requires (According to recent Apple documentation):

  • iOS 17, iPadOS 17, or macOS Sonoma (or later)
  • iPhone 12 or later
  • iPad Air (5th generation) or later
  • iPad Pro 11-inch (3rd generation) or later
  • iPad Pro 12.9-inch (5th generation) or later
  • Mac with Apple silicon
  • Face ID, Touch ID, or passcode enabled

Available in English and Mandarin Chinese (China mainland).

Switch Control

Use adaptive switches, head movements, or screen taps.

Requires: iOS 13 / iPadOS 13 or newer.

Voice Control

Control the entire device using spoken commands.

Requires: iOS 13 / iPadOS 13 or newer.

AssistiveTouch

On-screen button for gestures, actions, device controls.

Requires: iOS 10 / iPadOS 10 or newer.

Head Pointer / Tracking

Use head movements to control pointer or apps.

Requires: Built-in pointer needs iOS 15+ & TrueDepth camera (iPhone X+, iPad Pro 2018+). App-specific needs vary.

Android Features

Switch Access

Use adaptive switches to navigate and select.

Requires: Android Accessibility Suite (Android 5+).

Voice Access

Control the device using spoken commands.

Requires: "Voice Access" app from Google Play (Android 5+).

Other Android Accessibility

Includes TalkBack, magnification, font/display size, color/contrast options. Check device settings.

Note: Native eye tracking often needs third-party solutions on Android.

Quick Tips for Choosing/Using Devices

  • Prioritize Recent OS: Aim for devices supporting the latest OS (iPadOS 18+, iOS 18+, Android 14+) for best compatibility and features.
  • Check RAM/Processor: More RAM (4GB+) & faster chips help complex AAC apps run smoothly.
  • Storage: 64GB minimum recommended, 128GB+ is safer for lots of voices/media.
  • Backup Regularly: Always back up AAC vocabulary, especially before OS updates!
  • Consult Professionals: Talk to your SLP or AT Specialist for personalized device recommendations.

Device Unsupported? What Now?

If your device is old or no longer supported:

  • Contact your SLP/AT Team: Discuss options and next steps.
  • Explore Funding: Check insurance, waivers (Medicaid), school districts, or grant options for a new device.
  • Consider Refurbished: Look for certified refurbished newer models from reputable sellers (like Apple).
  • Simplify?: See if a simpler AAC app or layout might still work on the older device temporarily.
  • Backup Vocabulary: Ensure you have a recent backup of the user's language system!
